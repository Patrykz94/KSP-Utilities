Resonant Orbit
A resonant orbit is usually used when deploying satellite constellations. E.g. When deploying 4 satellites, the resonant orbit period should ideally be 1/4 shorter or 1/4 longer than the final orbital period. Satellites would be deployed once per orbit and circularise. That way they would be perfectly spaced out 90 degrees from each other.
